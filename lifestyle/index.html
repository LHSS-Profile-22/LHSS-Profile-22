Interests and lifestyle

General

Exercise

Almost 80% of students regularly exercise every month. Although school can be very time consuming, most students actively set aside time for physical activities.

Second half. Bar chart.

Cooking

Over half of students cook every week. In other words, many of us have a lot of experience cooking. How good our cooking is? That’s a completely different question that may be better left unanswered.

Second half. Bar chart.

Sleeping

Although software engineers may have a reputation for being nocturnal, most of us aren’t night owls. In fact, 72% of students usually sleep before 1AM. However, many students do still prefer sleeping late, possibly because they can focus better in the silence, or simply because they like gaming very late.

Second half. Distribution chart.

Extracurriculars

Unlike high school, almost half of students were not involved in any extracurriculars during undergrad. Amongst those that were involved, almost every student were the only SE 2018 student in their respective extracurricular activity.

Second half. Word cloud.

Coding

Programming language

Python is the most popular programming language, followed by Java, and then JavaScript.

Python’s versatility for web, scripting, and even machine learning could explain why it’s so popular in our class. Java continues to be very popular since it’s commonly taught in high school. And many companies hire web developers for co-op, which could explain why JavaScript, a scripting language for the web, is so popular.

Second half. Horizontal bar chart

Editor

Sublime Text is the most popular text editor used in our class, followed by Vim and Atom. Interestingly, Visual Studio Code is one of the least popular editors in our class but it is the most popular editor according to a StackOverflow survey.

Second half. Horizontal bar chart

Desktop OS

The majority of students are using a macOS machine. Interestingly, most students didn’t have a macOS machine in first year. This result isn’t surprising since macOS is built on top of Unix, enabling a lot of important functionality for programmers

Second half. Pie chart

Hackathons OS

More than 80% of students have gone to at least one hackathon, an overnight programming event where attendees build something from scratch.

Side projects

Most students have also worked on some sort of side project during university. Amongst those that have built side projects, only 25% dedicated more than 10 hours a month on their side projects.

Second half. Distribution

Discplines

57% of students enjoy an aspect of web development (front-end, back-end, full-stack). This could be explained by the positions available for our co-op terms, since many of them were related to web development.

After web development, 25% of students enjoy machine learning (ML) or artificial intelligence (AI). This aligns with the rising popularity of both disciplines.

Only 3 students have expressed interest in mobile development. This is surprising because of both how high in demand it is and how ubiquitous it is amongst consumers.
