Demographic & Background

Gender

Women make up 21% of students, which is less than the percent of women in tech (25%). The class saw a 3% increase in women representation from 1A to 4B, in first year, 18% of the class were female.

There are many on-campus communities that supports the success of women in computer science and engineering at UWaterloo. For example, Women in Software (WiSE), Women in Computer Science (WiCS), Women in Engineering (WiE), and Women Who Code Waterloo .

International Students, Immigration and Home Location

International Students

8.4% of respondents are international students, however, according to the official headcount 12.9% of the students in the class are international. The class saw a 2.1% decrease in international students from 1A (15%) to 4B. This could be due to the higher tuition costs for international students, especially in Engineering. International term tuition fees, prior to the academic year 2018/19 where it was subject to a 62.1% fee increase, were around $15000 for computer science students. This made the computer science program more cost beneficial to international students.

International students came from countries such as China and India.

Proportion of international students

Immigrated to Canada

35.7% of students, or 39% of domestic students immigrated to Canada. Immigrated students are any students who are considered domestic students and do not pay as a result international tuition, and among others are naturalized citizens or are Permanent Residents of Canada. Immigrated students came from countries including Belgium, China, England and India.

High School, Enriched Programs & CEGEP

Admission Average

The OSS average for the class was 96.5% and the OSS 10th decile was 94.3%. Although it is recommended to have a high 90 average to get accepted into the program, 11.3% of respondents had an average less than 95%.

Admission Average

Enriched Programs

Just over half of the respondents did not take part in an Enriched Program. The most popular Enriched Programs among respondents were AP and IB.

Students & Enriched Programs

CEGEP

All students from Quebec attended CEGEP partially, meaning they did not graduate. Among respondents, no students from outisde of Quebec attended CEGEP.

Quebec Students & CEGEP

CEGEP Fields

The students who attended CEGEP took part in a wide variety of programs related to Science, Mathematics, and Computer Science. Unlike other universities, CEGEP students, enrolled in an Engineering program at Waterloo, cannot convert any of their course taken in CEGEP into university credits, except for Calculus courses which can be counted as a credit toward Math 117 (Calculus 1 for Engineering).
