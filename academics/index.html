Attendance

Attendance consistently drops term by term, except after the hardest term. Either students enjoyed their 4A classes more, or they were possibly looking to compensate for their lower grades in 3B.

Alternatively, students attend slightly more class more during summer terms (2B and 4A).

Courses

Favourite required courses

Although 3B was the most difficult term, it had Concurrency (CS343), the most popular course. The top 8 favourited courses were all between 2A and 3B. Although these terms are slightly more difficult, they also happen to have the most interesting content for students.

Students are also required to take several technical electives to fulfill their credits. The most popular electives included Graphics (CS488), Distributed Computing (ECE 454), and Human-Computer Interaction (CS 449).

Least favourite required courses

3B also had Feedback Controls (SE 380), the most disliked course, which explains why it was the hardest term. Although SE 463 is just as disfavoured, it was a much easier course, which explains why 4A was an easier term.

It's important to note that courses that were disliked do not imply that students find the content useless. Instead, there are a variety of factors such as personal interests, final grade, assignments. For example, Logic and Computation (SE 212) is considered as useful to 88% of students according to UWFlow.

Attendance

There was no correlation between self-reported attendance and grades. To be clear, this does not conclude nor suggest that not going to class is beneficial. Instead, it suggests that it is possible to achieve good grades without attending all classes.
